● MedRad Clinics — Wireframe-Ready Homepage Structure
                                                                                                                                                            
  ---                                                                                                                                                       
  How to Read This Document                                                                                                                                 
  
  Each section is defined in order from top to bottom of the page. Layout logic uses simple column/row language — no code, no framework assumptions.          Interaction notes describe behavior only. Mobile notes describe how the layout shifts on narrow viewports.
                                                                                                                                                            
  ---
  Section 01 — Navigation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Purpose: Global wayfinding. Persistent conversion anchor. Never competes with page content.                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Layout Logic:                                                                                                                                               Single horizontal bar. Full width. Three zones:                                                                                                           
  - Left: Logo (text or mark, left-aligned)
  - Center: Four nav links — Services · Procedures · Education · About
  - Right: Book Appointment — contained button, restrained size, not oversized

  Fixed/sticky — stays at top of viewport on scroll. Thin bar, minimal height. No background shadow until scroll begins, then a subtle lift (very light     
  shadow or slight background opacity shift).

  Key Content Elements:
  - MedRad logo
  - 4 nav links (no dropdowns on hover — links go directly to section or page)
  - Book Appointment button
  - No utility nav, no login, no search in primary bar

  Interaction:
  - On scroll: nav gains a subtle background (white or near-white) to remain legible over page content
  - Book Appointment button state: default → hover (slight darkening, no animation)
  - Mobile menu trigger: opens a full-screen or slide-in panel with all nav links + Book CTA

  Mobile:
  - Logo left, hamburger icon right — no center nav links visible
  - Book Appointment removed from nav bar on mobile
  - Persistent bottom bar replaces it: full-width Book Appointment button, fixed to bottom of viewport throughout entire scroll
  - Mobile menu: full-screen overlay, large tap targets, nav links stacked vertically, Book CTA at bottom of menu panel

  ---
  Section 02 — First Screen (Entry Experience)

  Purpose: Establish voice, deliver the value proposition, present two entry points. Resolved within 3 seconds. No scrolling required to understand the     
  page.

  Layout Logic:
  Full viewport height (100vh). Two content zones stacked vertically on mobile, optionally side-by-side on large desktop:

  Zone A — Statement block
  Left-aligned or centered. Upper portion of viewport. Generous top padding. Contains the statement and attribution only.

  Zone B — Entry block
  Below Zone A, vertically separated by meaningful whitespace. Contains the two entry points and the passive permission line.

  No background image required. If imagery is used: single right-side or full-bleed background at low opacity — real clinic environment, soft lighting.     
  Typography must remain fully legible — no image should compete with the statement.

  Key Content Elements:

  Zone A:
  - Headline: "Before you book, let's make sure you're booking the right thing."
  — Largest typographic element on the page. Strong weight. Generous line height.
  - Attribution: Dr. E — Radiologist, MedRad Clinics
  — Small. Below headline. Not bold. Treated as a signature, not a subheading.

  Zone B:
  - Primary entry: Assess Your Risk →
  — Large. Typographic button or styled link. Dominant visual weight.
  - Secondary entry: I have a referral — book now
  — Smaller. Clearly present but visually subordinate. Same line or directly below primary.
  - Passive permission: Not sure? Scroll to explore.
  — Smallest text on the screen. Below both entries. No underline, no arrow icon. Quiet.

  Interaction:
  - Assess Your Risk → — expands the tool inline below (does not navigate away, does not open modal)
  - I have a referral — book now — navigates to booking flow (separate page or anchor to Section 07)
  - Scroll to explore — no interaction, purely permissive copy. May have a faint downward indicator if needed, but not required.

  Mobile:
  - Full-width single column
  - Headline at top, slightly smaller type size but still dominant
  - Both entry points stacked vertically, full-width tap targets
  - Passive permission line centered below
  - Bottom booking bar already present and fixed — no CTA duplication needed here

  ---
  Section 03 — Risk Assessment Tool (Inline Expansion)

  Purpose: The tool is not a separate page. It expands within the homepage when triggered. Three questions maximum, one at a time. Outputs a personalized   
  next step.

  Layout Logic:
  Expands below the entry block when Assess Your Risk → is selected. Pushes page content down — does not overlay or modal. Clean single-column container,   
  centered, max-width constrained (not full bleed). Generous padding on all sides.

  One question visible at a time. Previous questions collapse or fade to a minimal answered state above the current question. No visible form. No submit    
  button until after the final question.

  Key Content Elements:

  Q1 — "Where is the concern?"
  - 6 region options: Head/Neck · Chest · Abdomen · Spine · Limbs · Other
  - Layout: 2×3 tap-target grid on mobile, horizontal strip on desktop
  - No icons — text labels only

  Q2 — "How long has this been present?"
  - 4 options: Less than a month · 1–6 months · More than 6 months · Not sure
  - Layout: 2×2 on mobile, horizontal row on desktop

  Q3 (conditional — shown only if no referral detected) — "Have you seen a doctor about this yet?"
  - 3 options: Yes, with referral · Yes, no referral · Not yet
  - Layout: stacked on mobile, horizontal on desktop

  Output Block — appears after Q3:
  - Summary statement (2–3 sentences, calm editorial tone, no diagnosis language)
  - Dr. E note: 2-sentence contextual remark attributed to Dr. E — same typographic register as the summary, not a callout box
  - Relevant service: one service name, linked to its inner page
  - CTA: Book a consultation → — primary weight, same style as the entry point CTA above
  - Secondary link: Ask Dr. E a question — quiet, below the CTA

  Interaction:
  - Tap/click a region tile → Q1 collapses to a one-line answered state, Q2 appears with a subtle transition (fade or slide — no bounce, no spring
  animation, consistent with calm direction)
  - Same for Q2 → Q3
  - After Q3: output block appears below with a smooth reveal
  - No loading state required (no back-end call at this stage unless tool is live)
  - If user wants to change an answer: answered state is tappable to re-expand that question

  Mobile:
  - Full-width container, edge-to-edge padding reduced
  - Tap targets minimum 48px height
  - One question fully visible without scrolling
  - Output block stacks vertically: summary → Dr. E note → service → CTA

  ---
  Section 04 — Differentiator Line

  Purpose: Orient passive scrollers. Communicate what makes MedRad distinct in one statement. Earns the visitor's continued attention before asking for     
  anything.

  Layout Logic:
  Full-width typographic block. No background color, no border, no imagery. Centered or left-aligned text. Generous top and bottom padding — this section   
  breathes. It does not fight for space.

  Key Content Elements:
  - Single statement: "Most radiology clinics give you an appointment. We help you understand why you need one."
  - No subtext. No supporting copy. No CTA.

  Interaction: None. Static.

  Mobile:
  - Same treatment, full-width
  - Slightly reduced type size but maintained hierarchy
  - Padding reduced proportionally — still generous relative to surrounding sections

  ---
  Section 05 — Services by Intent

  Purpose: Route the passive browser to the right place. Organized by visitor intent, not by clinic catalogue. No card grid — structured list format.       

  Layout Logic:
  Two distinct intent groups, vertically stacked on mobile, optionally side-by-side on desktop with clear visual separation between groups. Each group has a
   short label and its corresponding items below it. No borders between items, no card containers. Clean list typographically differentiated from the group 
  label.

  Key Content Elements:

  Group 1 — "Referred for imaging"
  MRI · CT · Ultrasound · X-Ray · Mammography · PET
  Each item is a link. No descriptions. No icons.

  Group 2 — "Investigating symptoms"
  → Assess Your Risk
  Single link. Brings user back to the tool above (smooth scroll) or re-triggers the tool inline.

  Group 3 — "Following up on results"
  → Ask Dr. E · → Teaching Files
  Two links. Brief — one line.

  Interaction:
  - Each service name links to its individual service/procedure page
  - Assess Your Risk link scrolls back to Section 03 or re-triggers the tool
  - Ask Dr. E and Teaching Files link to their respective destination pages

  Mobile:
  - Three groups stacked vertically, full-width
  - Service names in Group 1: horizontal wrapping list or 2-column layout
  - Group 2 and 3: single full-width lines
  - Tap targets: full row height, not just the text width

  ---
  Section 06 — Dr. E Authority Layer

  Purpose: Establish a named human voice behind the site. Surface Ask Dr. E and Teaching Files without a dedicated education section. Build personal trust  
  before the testimonial.

  Layout Logic:
  Two-column on desktop: photograph left (roughly 40% width), text block right (60%). Stacked on mobile: photo top, text below. Photo is not full-bleed —   
  contained within its column, vertically centered or top-aligned. Significant whitespace around the entire section.

  Key Content Elements:

  Left column:
  - Single photograph of Dr. E
  - Real clinic environment, soft lighting, not posed
  - No caption, no name overlay on photo

  Right column:
  - Name: Dr. E — strong typographic weight
  - One credential line: title, specialization, institution if applicable — small, not bold
  - Statement (3 sentences maximum, Dr. E's direct voice):
  "Radiology reports are written for doctors. Most patients leave with results they can't read. Ask Dr. E exists because that gap shouldn't exist."
  - Two links below, quiet weight, separated by a thin space or soft divider:
  Ask Dr. E → · Explore Teaching Files →

  Interaction:
  - Ask Dr. E → navigates to the Ask Dr. E feature page
  - Explore Teaching Files → navigates to the Teaching Files section
  - No hover states on the photo
  - Links: standard hover state (subtle underline or opacity shift)

  Mobile:
  - Photo: full-width, cropped to square or 4:3, top of section
  - Text block below, full-width, left-aligned
  - Both links stacked vertically, full-width tap targets

  ---
  Section 07 — Patient Narrative

  Purpose: Social proof without a carousel. One real patient voice, editorial weight. Validates trust established by Dr. E layer above.

  Layout Logic:
  Full-width typographic block. No card border. No background color. No photo. Text only — centered or left-aligned with strong left margin on desktop. The 
  statement is the visual element. Quotation mark may be used as a large typographic anchor if consistent with visual direction, but not required.

  Key Content Elements:
  - Patient narrative (4–5 sentences, written in first person):
  "I came in with a scan referral I didn't understand. Before I called, I used the assessment tool and realized I had questions I hadn't thought to ask. By 
  the time I arrived, I knew what I was there for."
  - Attribution line below: Patient name · Procedure type · City — small, restrained
  - Google aggregate (optional, secondary): 4.9 · 214 Google reviews → — single line, below attribution, linked to Google Business profile

  Interaction:
  - No carousel controls — this is one static story
  - Google aggregate is a link — opens Google reviews in new tab
  - If multiple stories exist: rotate at content level (editorial decision) not UI level — no carousel

  Mobile:
  - Full-width, generous padding
  - Attribution and Google line stacked
  - No layout change required — already single column

  ---
  Section 08 — Procedures Reference Strip

  Purpose: Surface the Procedures & Tests feature without a full section. Pre-appointment utility for visitors who know they need imaging. Minimal
  footprint.

  Layout Logic:
  Single horizontal line on desktop. Preceded by a short label. No section header, no background, no card. Sits between the patient narrative and booking   
  close as a quiet utility band.

  Key Content Elements:
  - Label: "Preparing for a scan?" — small, restrained weight
  - Procedure links on one line: MRI · CT · Ultrasound · Mammogram · PET · X-Ray · Interventional
  - Each item links to its individual procedure page

  Interaction:
  - Each procedure name is a link
  - Hover: subtle underline or color shift only

  Mobile:
  - Label stacks above
  - Procedure names: horizontal scroll strip (overflow-x scroll, no wrapping)
  - Scroll indicator: faint fade on right edge to suggest more content — no scrollbar visible

  ---
  Section 09 — Booking Close

  Purpose: Convert visitors who have reached the bottom of the page. Re-entry point for those who scrolled without using the tool. Final CTA before the     
  footer.

  Layout Logic:
  Two sub-blocks, vertically stacked, full-width. Clean separation from the section above via whitespace only — no divider line needed.

  Sub-block A — CTA:
  Centered or left-aligned. Short label above the button. One primary action. One secondary action below it.

  Sub-block B — Location/Contact:
  Below CTA block. Clinic name, address, phone number, operating hours. Simple text layout. No map embed required at wireframe stage — placeholder space    
  defined.

  Key Content Elements:

  Sub-block A:
  - Label: "Ready to take the next step?"
  - Primary CTA: Book an Appointment → — full-width on mobile, constrained width on desktop
  - Secondary link below: Or start with Assess Your Risk → — quiet, text-link weight

  Sub-block B:
  - Clinic name
  - Street address
  - Phone number
  - Operating hours (weekday / weekend if different)
  - Map placeholder (optional at this stage)

  Interaction:
  - Book an Appointment → navigates to booking flow
  - Assess Your Risk → scrolls to Section 03 or re-triggers the tool
  - Phone number: tel: link on mobile (tap to call)

  Mobile:
  - Both sub-blocks full-width, stacked
  - CTA button: full-width tap target
  - Location details: stacked line by line, legible at small size
  - Bottom booking bar (from nav) overlaps this section — ensure CTA button does not conflict visually with the fixed bar; add bottom padding equal to bar  
  height

  ---
  Section 10 — Footer

  Purpose: Navigation safety net. Legal compliance. Secondary access to all features. Login/Register lives here.

  Layout Logic:
  Multi-column on desktop (3–4 columns). Stacked single column on mobile. Standard footer structure — not a design feature.

  Key Content Elements:
  - Column 1: Logo + short descriptor line (1 sentence)
  - Column 2: Site navigation — all primary pages
  - Column 3: Features — Assess Your Risk · Ask Dr. E · Teaching Files · Case Gallery · Procedures
  - Column 4: Contact + social links (Instagram, Facebook, Google)
  - Bottom bar: Copyright · Privacy Policy · Terms · Accessibility · Login / Register

  Interaction: Standard link behavior. No hover effects required beyond underline.

  Mobile:
  - Columns stack vertically
  - Each column collapsible (accordion) optional — depends on footer depth
  - Bottom bar wraps to two lines if needed
