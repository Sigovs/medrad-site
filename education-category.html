Interventions

The venous system returns blood to the heart — and when veins become diseased, damaged, or obstructed, interventional radiology offers targeted, minimally invasive treatments that restore flow, relieve symptoms, and protect long-term vascular health. Venous Procedures & Treatments

Revascularization of Central Venous Occlusions

When central veins become blocked — often from catheters, clots, or radiation — percutaneous revascularization restores flow and relieves upper-body swelling, arm pain, and port dysfunction.

Procedure Guide Read article →

Inferior Vena Cava (IVC) Filters

A small device placed inside the inferior vena cava to intercept blood clots before they reach the lungs. Used when anticoagulation is contraindicated or insufficient to prevent pulmonary embolism.

Procedure Guide Read article →

Sclerotherapy of Varicose & Spider Veins

A sclerosing agent is injected directly into diseased veins, causing them to close and gradually fade. An effective, well-tolerated treatment for varicose veins, spider veins, and venous malformations.

Overview Read article →

Phlebectomy of Varicose Veins

Minimally invasive removal of superficial varicose veins through tiny skin punctures — under local anesthesia, with no significant scarring. Most patients return to normal activity within days.

Procedure Guide Read article →

Endovenous Laser Therapy (EVLT)

A laser fiber threaded into the diseased vein delivers targeted energy that closes the vein from within. EVLT treats chronic venous insufficiency of the lower extremities with minimal downtime and high long-term success rates.

Venous Access for Chemotherapy & Long-Term IV Therapy

Port-a-caths, PICCs, and tunneled catheters provide reliable, long-term intravenous access for chemotherapy, nutrition, and frequent blood draws — all placed and maintained with image guidance.

Overview Read article →

Declotting of AV Fistulas & Grafts

For dialysis patients, a clotted arteriovenous fistula or graft can interrupt life-sustaining treatment. Image-guided declotting rapidly restores access through pharmacomechanical thrombolysis — without surgery.

Hemodialysis Access — Creation & Maintenance

MedRad Clinics manages the full lifecycle of hemodialysis access — from initial catheter placement through fistula and graft creation, long-term surveillance, and intervention — ensuring uninterrupted treatment for patients with kidney disease.

Patient Guidance

What to Expect

Before

Preparation

You will receive procedure-specific instructions in advance. This typically includes fasting guidelines, medication adjustments (especially blood thinners), and arranging transport home. Bring a complete list of your current medications.

During

The Procedure

Venous procedures are performed under ultrasound or fluoroscopic guidance with local anesthesia and light sedation. A small skin puncture provides access — no surgical incision required. Most procedures are completed in under two hours.

After

Recovery

Many patients return home the same day. Activity may be restricted for 24–48 hours depending on the specific procedure. Follow-up imaging confirms treatment success. Written aftercare instructions are provided before discharge.

Most venous interventional procedures are performed under local anesthesia and light sedation. You may feel pressure or a warm sensation during the procedure but significant pain is uncommon. Your care team will monitor your comfort throughout and can adjust sedation as needed.

Recovery varies by procedure. For sclerotherapy and phlebectomy, most patients resume normal activities within 24–48 hours. More complex procedures such as central vein revascularization or hemodialysis access creation may require a short overnight stay and a few days of restricted activity. Your physician will give you specific guidance based on your procedure.

A referral from your primary care physician or specialist is helpful but not always required. We accept self-referrals for consultation. Please bring any relevant imaging studies (ultrasound, CT, MRI) and a list of current medications to your first appointment. Our team will review your case and recommend the most appropriate treatment path.

The right procedure depends on the nature and location of the venous problem, your overall health, and your goals. During your consultation, Dr. Ekechukwu will review your imaging and clinical history, explain all applicable options, and make a recommendation. There is no obligation — the consultation is an opportunity to ask questions and make an informed decision.

Coverage depends on your specific medical aid scheme and the clinical indication for the procedure. Medically necessary procedures — such as IVC filter placement, AV fistula declotting, or treatment of deep vein thrombosis — are typically covered. Elective or cosmetic procedures such as spider vein sclerotherapy may not be. Our administrative team can assist with pre-authorisation and provide detailed billing codes for your insurer.
